# Retail Sales Intelligence Dashboard

## Business Insights & Strategic Recommendations

### Executive Summary

This project analyzes over 25,000 retail orders from a global retail organization using SQL and Power BI. The objective was to identify key revenue drivers, profitability patterns, category performance, regional efficiency, and product-level risks. The analysis revealed significant opportunities to improve profitability through better category management, regional optimization, and product portfolio decisions.

---

# 1. Revenue Performance by Region

## Finding

| Region         | Revenue    |
| -------------- | ---------- |
| Central        | $2,818,863 |
| South          | $1,598,169 |
| North          | $1,248,166 |
| Oceania        | $1,100,185 |
| Southeast Asia | $884,423   |

## Business Insight

The Central region is the primary revenue driver, contributing significantly more revenue than any other region. South and North regions also demonstrate strong sales performance, indicating that these markets represent the company's most important revenue-generating territories.

The concentration of revenue within a few regions suggests that regional performance has a substantial impact on overall business results.

## Recommendation

* Prioritize customer retention and market expansion initiatives in Central and South regions.
* Allocate additional resources to support high-performing regions.
* Develop targeted growth strategies for lower-revenue regions.

# 2. Profit Performance by Region

## Finding

| Region         | Profit   |
| -------------- | -------- |
| Central        | $311,827 |
| North          | $194,598 |
| North Asia     | $165,578 |
| South          | $139,642 |
| Southeast Asia | $132,480 |

## Business Insight

Although Central leads both revenue and profit generation, profitability varies considerably across regions. North Asia demonstrates strong profitability despite generating lower revenue than South and Oceania.

This indicates that some regions operate more efficiently and generate greater profit per dollar of sales.

## Recommendation

* Study operational practices in North Asia and North regions.
* Identify pricing and cost-management strategies driving stronger profitability.
* Replicate successful practices across lower-margin regions.

# 3. Regional Profit Margin Analysis

## Finding

| Region       | Profit Margin |
| ------------ | ------------- |
| Canada       | 26.62%        |
| North Asia   | 19.52%        |
| Central Asia | 17.60%        |
| North        | 15.59%        |
| West         | 14.86%        |
| East         | 13.49%        |
| Africa       | 11.34%        |
| Central      | 11.06%        |
| Oceania      | 10.92%        |
| Caribbean    | 10.66%        |

## Business Insight

Revenue leadership does not necessarily translate into operational efficiency.

While Central generates the highest revenue and profit, its profit margin remains only 11.06%. In contrast, Canada achieves the highest margin at 26.62%, demonstrating superior efficiency and profitability.

This suggests significant opportunities to improve margins within high-volume regions.

## Recommendation

* Investigate cost structures and discounting practices within Central and Oceania.
* Benchmark high-performing regions such as Canada and North Asia.
* Implement regional profitability monitoring dashboards.

# 4. Category Performance Analysis

## Finding

| Category        | Revenue    | Profit   |
| --------------- | ---------- | -------- |
| Technology      | $4,744,304 | $663,710 |
| Furniture       | $4,101,922 | $283,734 |
| Office Supplies | $3,771,526 | $516,473 |

## Business Insight

Technology is the strongest category overall, generating both the highest revenue and highest profit.

Furniture contributes substantial revenue but delivers significantly lower profitability relative to sales volume. This indicates pricing, discounting, or cost management challenges within the category.

Office Supplies demonstrates strong profitability despite lower overall revenue.

## Recommendation

* Increase investment in Technology products.
* Review pricing and discount policies within Furniture.
* Expand profitable Office Supplies product lines.

# 6. Loss-Making Product Analysis

## Finding

Top Loss-Making Products:

1. Cubify CubeX 3D Printer Double Head Print – -$8,879.97
2. Lexmark MX611dhe Monochrome Laser Printer – -$4,589.97
3. Motorola Smart Phone, Cordless – -$4,447.04
4. Cubify CubeX 3D Printer Triple Head Print – -$3,839.99
5. Bevis Round Table, Adjustable Height – -$3,649.89

## Business Insight

Several products generate substantial losses despite belonging to otherwise profitable categories.

This indicates that product-level profitability varies significantly and that revenue alone should not be used to evaluate performance.

The presence of highly negative-profit products suggests pricing, discounting, inventory, or operational inefficiencies.

## Recommendation

* Conduct SKU-level profitability reviews.
* Reassess pricing and discount strategies.
* Consider product redesign, supplier renegotiation, or discontinuation of consistently loss-making products.

---

# 7. Strategic Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

### Revenue Growth

* Expand operations in Central and South regions.
* Increase investment in Technology products.
* Develop customer retention strategies in key markets.

### Profitability Improvement

* Benchmark high-margin regions such as Canada and North Asia.
* Reduce operational inefficiencies in lower-margin regions.
* Strengthen profitability monitoring at regional and product levels.

### Product Optimization

* Increase focus on high-performing smartphone and technology products.
* Review underperforming furniture products.
* Eliminate or redesign persistently loss-making products.

### Decision Intelligence

* Implement executive dashboards for continuous monitoring.
* Establish profitability KPIs at region, category, and product levels.
* Introduce automated alerts for margin deterioration.

---

# Conclusion

The analysis demonstrates that while the company generates strong revenue across multiple global regions, substantial opportunities exist to improve profitability through regional optimization, category management, and product portfolio refinement.

Technology products and high-margin regions represent the strongest growth opportunities, while loss-making products and low-efficiency regions require immediate management attention.

A data-driven approach to pricing, product management, and regional strategy can significantly enhance overall business performance.
